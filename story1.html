Into The Techverse

Quote

Learn continually, there is always "one more thing" to learn.

Steve Jobs

If someone had told me two months ago that I would be able to build a website from scratch I would have laughed them out of town. As in hahaha to their face all the way to Kaabong. You see, I am of the analogue era and technology generally intimidates me. Or used to until very recently when out sheer curiosity and plenty of encouragement from my friends I signed up for a course in Software Engineering.

To say I was the most unlikely candidate for such a course would be the understatement of the year. My interaction with computers has always been limited to the MS office packages I learned many years ago, emails and “googling” stuff, you know, the basics. That black screen with its blinking cursor and unreadable words always gave me the heebie jeebies but yet when the opportunity to apply for the catalyst course at Refactory came up I jumped at it.

Once I got accepted, I knew I was going to do everything I could to learn because I don’t do half measure. And I’m learning, slower than I would have liked but learning all the same and enjoying it. I am not ‘of here’ any more! I KNOW STUFF. I know how the internet works, I know how content gets to my screen every time I ‘Ask Google’ something. I know what’s happening in the background as I look at a website or shop on-line. Fascinating stuff I tell you.

The first two weeks were about the basics of programming, downloading the tools we would need and introductions to a whole bunch of things I had never heard of like GIT, HTML and CSS. All which was like greek to me when we started out but I have since grown comfortable enough with them to build this website and I’m over the moon! I am constantly impressed by just how much work goes into even the simplest tech.

What started out as a curiosity has turned into a hunger to discover more of this strange universe I find myself in. It has become a rabbit hole of black screens, elements, attributes and tags, the more I learn, the more I want to know. This is the kind of self indulging I signed up for, so into the techverse I go…
